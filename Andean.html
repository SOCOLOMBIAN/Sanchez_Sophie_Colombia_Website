Andean Region

The Andean region is at the heart of Colombia, home to towering mountains, vast valleys, and a rich cultural heritage. Dominated by the Andes mountain range, this region offers dramatic landscapes and a wide variety of outdoor activities such as hiking, mountain biking, and birdwatching. With beautiful colonial cities, ancient archaeological sites, and colorful markets, the Andean region is perfect for travelers looking for a mix of nature, history, and adventure.

Tourism destinations in the Andean

Cundinamarca

Monserrate

Monserrate is a mountain that towers over Bogotá, offering panoramic views of the city. The summit is home to a church and a pilgrimage site, making it both a spiritual and scenic destination.

activities

  • Take the cable car to the top
  • Enjoy the views of Bogotá
  • Visit the church at the summit

Plaza Mayor

One of the largest cobbled squares in Colombia, Plaza Mayor is the heart of Villa de Leyva. Surrounded by colonial architecture, it's a perfect place to relax, have a coffee, and admire the town's charm.

Activities:

  • monkey interactions
  • boats tours around the inslands

Nemocón Salt Mine

Another fascinating salt mine, the Nemocón Salt Mine offers an educational and immersive experience. Visitors can walk through the tunnels, see the salt crystals, and even learn about the ancient techniques used for mining.

Activities:

  • Take a guided tour of the mine
  • Marvel at the salt formations
  • Learn about the mining history

Santander

Chicamocha National Park

A must-visit destination for nature lovers and thrill-seekers. This park offers breathtaking views of the Chicamocha Canyon, one of the largest canyons in the world. You can enjoy activities like zip-lining, paragliding, and rafting.

Activities:

  • Zip-lining across the canyon
  • Rafting on the Chicamocha River
  • Paragliding

San Gil Town

Known as the adventure capital of Colombia, San Gil offers thrilling activities like caving, hiking, and horseback riding, along with a beautiful colonial center filled with cobblestone streets and traditional markets.

Activities:

  • Explore the historic town center
  • Hiking to the Juan Curí Waterfalls
  • Horseback riding in the surrounding mountains

Barichara

Barichara is a charming colonial village often described as one of the most beautiful in Colombia. Its cobbled streets, whitewashed buildings, and surrounding hills create a picturesque setting perfect for walking tours and cultural exploration.

Activities:

  • Explore the cobbled streets and whitewashed buildings
  • Visit the impressive Catedral de la Inmaculada Concepción
  • Hike to the Guane archaeological site
