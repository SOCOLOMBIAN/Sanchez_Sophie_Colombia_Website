Caribean Region

The Caribbean Coast of Colombia is a vibrant and diverse region, known for its stunning beaches, colonial architecture, and rich cultural history. With a tropical climate and beautiful landscapes, it offers a variety of experiences, from bustling cities to tranquil islands.

Map of the Caribean region

Tourist destinations in the Caribbean

Atlantico

Museo del oro

The Museo del Oro in Barranquilla is home to a rich collection of pre-Colombian gold artifacts and indigenous art. The museum tells the story of Colombia's ancient cultures and their craftsmanship.

activities

  • Explore pre-Colombian artifacts and learn about Colombia's ancient civilizations.
  • Attend guided tours that provide deeper insight into the goldwork and rituals of indigenous cultures.
  • Take photographs of the stunning gold pieces and exhibits.

Carnaval de Barranquilla

The Carnaval of Barranquilla is one of the largest and most important festivals in Colombia, recognized by UNESCO. It's a vibrant celebration of music, dance, costumes, and tradition.

Activities:

  • Watch the colorful **Parade of the Carnival** with music, dance, and spectacular costumes.
  • Join the street parties and experience the lively atmosphere of Barranquilla's famous Carnival.
  • Learn traditional Caribbean dances like **Cumbia** and **Mapalé** from locals.

Colombian Port

Puerto Colombia Beach is one of the most popular beaches near Barranquilla. It offers golden sands, calm waters, and excellent facilities for relaxation and water activities.

Activities:

  • Relax on the beach and enjoy the Caribbean sun.
  • Try water sports like jet skiing, windsurfing, or paddleboarding.
  • Enjoy seafood at beachfront restaurants with fresh catches of the day.

Magdalena

Tayrona National Park

Tayrona National Park is one of Colombia's most beautiful and biodiverse parks, offering pristine beaches, jungle hiking trails, and the chance to see native wildlife in their natural habitat.

activities

  • Hike through the jungle to reach stunning beaches like Cabo San Juan.
  • Go wildlife spotting, with opportunities to see monkeys, iguanas, and exotic birds.
  • Snorkel in crystal-clear waters and explore coral reefs off the park's beaches.

Lost City

The Lost City (Ciudad Perdida) is an ancient indigenous city hidden in the Sierra Nevada mountains. It requires a multi-day hike to reach, offering an adventurous journey into Colombia's past.

Activities:

  • Take a guided trek through the dense jungle to reach the Lost City.
  • Learn about the Tayrona civilization and their culture from local guides.
  • Enjoy panoramic views of the Sierra Nevada mountains and surrounding jungle landscapes.

Tangana Beach

Taganga Beach is a laid-back fishing village near Santa Marta. It's known for its relaxed vibe, diving opportunities, and beautiful sunsets.

Activities:

  • Go diving or snorkeling in the vibrant coral reefs off the coast.
  • Take a boat trip to nearby beaches like Playa Grande or Bahía Concha.
  • Watch the sunset over the bay from a local café or restaurant.
